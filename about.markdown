## Hi, I'm Jiyoung.

I'm a 4th-year PhD student at the [KAIST Graduate School of AI](https://gsai.kaist.ac.kr/), advised by Professor [Edward Choi](https://mp2893.com/). My work sits at the intersection of **AI evaluation, human-AI alignment, and social/linguistic diversity**.

## Research Philosophy

I believe that before an AI model is deployed to the public, it should be rigorously assessed — not only for accuracy, but for whether it aligns with the values, uncertainty, and diversity of the humans it will serve. This belief drives most of what I do:

- **Do AI models handle linguistic diversity fairly?** I study how large language models behave across dialects, non-standard English varieties, and low-resource linguistic communities.
- **Do AI models perceive the world the way people do?** I build benchmarks that test whether models can recognize uncertainty, ambiguity, and edge cases the way a thoughtful human would.
- **Are our benchmarks themselves trustworthy?** Constructing benchmarks with 6,000+ human participants has taught me that dataset design is a research problem in its own right — one that requires statistical rigor and cross-disciplinary collaboration.
